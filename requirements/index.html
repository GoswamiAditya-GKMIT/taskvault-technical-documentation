    System Requirements

    This section outlines the functional and non-functional requirements of the TaskVault system.

    Functional Requirements

    User Management

    • The system shall allow users to register and authenticate using JWT.
    • The system shall support two roles: Admin and User.
    • Users shall be able to update their own profile information.
    • Administrators shall be able to view, update, and soft delete any user.
    • Users shall be able to soft delete their own account.
    • Soft deletion of a user shall cascade to tasks and comments owned or assigned to the user.

    Task Management

    • Users shall be able to create tasks only for themselves.
    • Administrators shall be able to create tasks for themselves or assign tasks to users.
    • Each task shall include title, description, status, priority, and optional deadline.
    • Duplicate task titles for the same ownerâ€“assignee combination shall be prevented.
    • Users shall be able to view only tasks assigned to them or created by them.
    • Administrators shall be able to view all tasks.
    • Tasks shall support filtering by owner, assignee, status, and priority.
    • Tasks shall be soft deleted instead of permanently removed.

    Task Updates and History

    • Task status, priority, and deadline shall be updatable.
    • Deadlines shall not be allowed in the past.
    • The system shall record immutable history entries for task status or priority changes.
    • Task history shall remain accessible even if the modifying user is deleted.

    Comments

    • Users shall be able to comment on tasks they own or are assigned to.
    • Administrators shall be able to comment only on tasks they own.
    • Users shall be able to update and delete their own comments.
    • Comments shall be soft deleted.

    Access Control

    • Role-based and object-level permissions shall be enforced for all protected endpoints.
    • Deleted users shall not be allowed to authenticate or access APIs.

    Non-Functional Requirements

    Security

    • All protected APIs shall require authentication.
    • Passwords shall be securely hashed.
    • JWT shall be used for stateless authentication.
    • Soft-deleted users shall be blocked from system access.

    Performance

    • Pagination shall be enabled for all list endpoints.
    • Indexed fields shall be used for frequently queried columns.
    • UUIDs shall be used for primary keys.

    Reliability and Maintainability

    • Soft deletion shall be used to prevent accidental data loss.
    • Audit logs shall be immutable.
    • Permission logic shall be centralized and reusable.
    • APIs shall follow consistent response structures.

    Usability and Portability

    • APIs shall follow RESTful design principles.
    • PATCH shall be used for partial updates.
